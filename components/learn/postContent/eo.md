What is EO?

EO stands for edge orientation. Every rubik's cube has 12 edge pieces. With edge orientation, we can define each edge as good or bad. An edge is defined as good if it can be completely solved with just R, L, U, and D moves.

Subtitle: Why is solving EO useful?

Once EO is solved, the entire cube can be solved with just those R, L, U, and D moves. Because you are a human with hands (citation needed), and because we've figured out ways to use our fingers to do U and D moves and our wrists to do L and R moves, and alternate between them, these can be very fast moves. And turning fast is half the battle.


Subtitle: EO recognition

Since simplifying the cube in this way is useful, we need strategies to recognize whether an edge is good or bad.

Recognizing the orientation of the edge gives information on how to solve that edge. This might not help you solve the cube **faster**, but it can make you more **efficient**. A good edge will never require you to:
1. Rotate the entire cube in order to solve it, or 
2. Use awkward F or B moves
These two limitations can make it easier to find a good way to solve the case because you will have fewer possibilities you'll need to check.
